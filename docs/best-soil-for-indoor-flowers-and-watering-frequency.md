# Best Soil for Indoor Flowers and Watering Frequency

Choosing the right soil and watering schedule is essential for healthy indoor flowers. This guide covers the basics.

## Best Soil for Indoor Flowers

- **General-purpose potting mix**: Most indoor flowers thrive in a well-draining, lightweight potting mix. Look for one that contains peat moss, perlite, and vermiculite.
- **Succulents and cacti**: Use a fast-draining, sandy mix to prevent root rot.
- **Orchids**: Require a bark-based mix that allows air circulation around roots.
- **African violets**: Prefer a light, porous mix with added perlite.
- **Self-made mix**: Combine equal parts peat moss, perlite, and compost for a balanced option.

## How Often to Water Indoor Flowers

Water frequency depends on the plant species, pot size, humidity, and season. General guidelines:

- **Check soil moisture**: Stick your finger about an inch into the soil. If it feels dry, it's time to water.
- **Common intervals**: Most indoor flowers need watering every 1–2 weeks in growing season, less in winter.
- **Overwatering signs**: Yellowing leaves, wilting, or mold on soil can indicate too much water.
- **Underwatering signs**: Drooping, brown leaf edges, and dry soil pulling away from pot.
- **Always use pots with drainage holes** to prevent waterlogging.

## Additional Tips

- Use room-temperature water to avoid shocking roots.
- Water thoroughly until water drains out the bottom, then discard excess.
- Group plants with similar watering needs together.
- Adjust frequency based on light and temperature – more light and heat increase evaporation.

By matching soil and watering to each plant's needs, you can enjoy vibrant, healthy indoor flowers year-round.
