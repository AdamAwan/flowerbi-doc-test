# Why a Magpie?

The Markdown Magpie name and mascot were chosen to evoke the project's core purpose: **collecting, curating, and maintaining knowledge** — just as the bird is known for gathering bright, interesting objects.

## The metaphor

- **Ingenuity & resourcefulness:** Magpies are intelligent corvids that solve problems and adapt. Magpie the tool adapts to any Markdown knowledge base, finds gaps, and proposes fixes.
- **Collecting what matters:** A magpie's nest is built from gathered treasures. Magpie gathers document sections, answers questions, and preserves what's valuable — filtering noise to keep knowledge bright.
- **Vocal and social:** Magpies are talkative, warning of change. Magpie flags low-confidence answers, surfaces gaps, and proposes improvements — keeping the team informed without shouting.

## Alignment with product values

The pitch deck anchors on three promises: **won't lie, won't leak, won't rot** (see `presentation/README.md`). A magpie, as a symbol of honest, grounded curation, fits naturally:

- **Won't lie** — answers are cited; gaps are honestly flagged.
- **Won't leak** — Git is the source of truth; nothing is lost.
- **Won't rot** — the continuous maintenance loop (gap detection → proposal → PR → merge) keeps knowledge fresh.

The magpie captures both the playful and reliable character of the tool — intelligent enough to find and fix what's missing, but always grounded in the repository it serves.
