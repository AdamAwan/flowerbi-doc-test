# Template Sales Pitch for Small Local Companies

This document provides a flexible, customisable sales-pitch template designed specifically for small local businesses. Use it as a starting point to communicate your value clearly and persuasively to local customers.

## When to Use This Template

- Cold outreach (phone, email, in-person)
- Warm introductions (referrals, networking events)
- Brief elevator pitches (under 60 seconds)
- Longer conversation openers (1–3 minutes)

## Key Principles for Local Pitches

1. **Start with the customer’s world** — mention their town, street, or local context first.
2. **Focus on outcomes, not features** — say how you save time, money, or hassle.
3. **Build trust fast** — cite other local customers or community ties.
4. **Keep it short** — respect their time; aim for 30 seconds unless they invite more.
5. **Have a low-friction next step** — free trial, no-obligation quote, or a simple follow-up.

## Core Pitch Template

Below is a three-part structure. Use the bracketed placeholders and then adapt the tone (friendly, professional, energetic) to your brand.

### 1. Hook (5–10 seconds)

> “Hi [Name], I’m [Your Name] from [Your Company]. We help [small businesses in City] [achieve X benefit].”

*Example:* “Hi Sarah, I’m Alex from GreenClean — we help local cafés in Bristol keep their windows spotless without disrupting your morning rush.”

### 2. Problem–Solution Bridge (10–15 seconds)

> “I know [common local problem] can be a pain. What we do is [your simple solution], and we’ve already done it for [local reference client].”

*Example:* “I know managing bookings and cancellations takes hours each week. What our booking system does is automate reminders and rebook cancelled slots instantly. We’ve set it up for the hairdresser on Main Street, and they saved 8 hours a month.”

### 3. Ask (5–10 seconds)

> “Would you be open to a [free 15-minute call / quick demo / no-obligation quote] next [day]? I’ll show you exactly how it works for your situation.”

*Example:* “Would you be open to a 15-minute call this Thursday? I’ll walk you through how your staff could use it in under a minute.”

## Full Example (Local IT Support Company)

> “Hi, I’m Jordan from CityTech. We help small law firms in Manchester keep their data secure without hiring a full-time IT person. I know that even a single ransomware attack can shut you down for days — our cloud backup and 24/7 monitoring catches threats before they cause damage. We look after three firms on Deansgate already. Could we grab a coffee next Tuesday? I’ll run a free five-minute health check on your network.”

## Adaptation Guidance

| Business Type | Tone | Key Benefit to Lead With |
|---------------|------|--------------------------|
| Service (plumber, cleaner) | Friendly, reliable | Fast response, local trust |
| Retail (café, boutique) | Warm, personal | Community feel, unique products |
| Professional (accountant, lawyer) | Trustworthy, expert | Confidentiality, compliance |
| Tech / SaaS | Efficient, modern | Time savings, automation |

## Email / Written Version

**Subject:** Quick idea for [Business Name]

**Body:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I’m [Your Name] from [Company]. We help [audience] in [location] [achieve benefit].
>
> I noticed [something relevant about their business] — many of our customers struggled with [problem] before working with us.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call next week? I’ll share a few ideas specific to [their business]. No obligation, and I can send over a one-page summary afterwards.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]
> [Phone / Website / Social link]

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Talking only about yourself** — lead with their need, not your history.
- **Being too vague** — name a specific problem or benefit.
- **Asking for too much too soon** — start with a low-commitment next step.
- **Forgetting the local angle** — mention local streets, landmarks, or community events.
- **Not practising out loud** — rehearse until it sounds natural, not robotic.

## Practice Checklist

- [ ] I can deliver the hook in one breath.
- [ ] I include a real local reference or example.
- [ ] The ask is clear and takes less than 15 minutes.
- [ ] I have a one-page sheet or link ready to send.
- [ ] I’ve tailored the tone to my audience.
